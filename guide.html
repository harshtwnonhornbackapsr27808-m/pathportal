📖 Overview

What Is MyChart?

MyChart is an online patient portal platform developed by Epic Systems. It is used by hundreds of hospitals, clinics, and healthcare organizations across the United States and internationally to give patients secure online access to their health information.

Through MyChart, patients are typically able to view records from their visits, communicate with their care team, schedule appointments, and manage other health-related tasks — all from a web browser or mobile device.

ℹ️

Important: PortalPath is not affiliated with MyChart or Epic Systems. This guide provides general informational overviews only and does not constitute official documentation from any portal provider.

🔑 Access

Accessing MyChart

MyChart is generally accessed through a web browser or through the MyChart mobile app. Because each healthcare organization runs its own version of MyChart, the specific web address varies depending on your provider.

1

Find Your Provider's MyChart Address

Your healthcare organization will have its own MyChart URL. This is usually shared in your welcome letter, appointment reminder, or the organization's official website.

2

Open the Access Screen

Navigate to your provider's MyChart address in any modern web browser. You will see a screen with fields for your username and password.

3

Enter Your Credentials

Type your MyChart username and password into the appropriate fields. These were set when you first created your account or were provided by your healthcare organization.

4

Complete Any Verification Step

Some MyChart portals require an additional verification step, such as entering a code sent to your phone or email, before granting access.

5

You Are In

Once authenticated, you will be taken to your MyChart home dashboard where you can navigate your portal features.

⚙️ Setup

Setting Up a MyChart Account

New MyChart accounts are typically created in one of two ways — through an activation code provided by your healthcare organization, or through an online self-signup option if your provider supports it.

1

Receive Your Activation Code

After a visit or upon enrollment, your healthcare provider typically gives you an activation code via letter, email, or an after-visit summary document.

2

Visit the Sign-Up Page

Go to your provider's MyChart website and look for a "Sign Up" or "Create Account" option, then enter your activation code when prompted.

3

Verify Your Identity

You will typically be asked to confirm some personal details — such as your date of birth — to verify your identity before proceeding.

4

Choose a Username and Password

Create a unique username and a strong password that meets the portal's requirements. Store these somewhere safe for future use.

5

Complete Setup and Sign In

Once your account is created, you can use your new credentials to access your MyChart portal whenever you need.

💡

If you did not receive an activation code, contact your healthcare provider directly. They can resend it or help you sign up through an alternative method.

🔄 Recovery

MyChart Account Recovery

If you are unable to access your MyChart account due to a forgotten username or password, most portals provide self-service recovery options directly on the access screen.

1

Go to Your Provider's MyChart Page

Navigate to the same MyChart address you would normally use to access your portal.

2

Look for "Forgot Username" or "Forgot Password"

On the access screen, there are typically links below the credential fields that allow you to begin the recovery process.

3

Provide Verification Information

You will be asked to enter details such as your email address, date of birth, or other information on file to confirm your identity.

4

Check Your Email or Phone

A recovery link or temporary code is typically sent to the email address or phone number associated with your account.

5

Reset and Regain Access

Follow the instructions in the recovery message to reset your password or retrieve your username, then use your updated credentials to sign in.

⚠️

If self-service recovery does not work — for example, if your email address on file is outdated — contact your healthcare provider's support team directly. They can verify your identity and restore access.

🗺️ Navigation

Navigating Your MyChart Portal

Once you are inside MyChart, the dashboard provides access to all of the portal's main sections. While the exact layout can vary by organization, most MyChart portals follow a similar structure.

1

The Home Dashboard

The main screen typically shows a summary of upcoming appointments, recent notifications, and quick-access buttons to the most common sections.

2

The Main Menu

A navigation menu — usually at the top or side of the screen — provides access to all major sections such as Appointments, Messages, Health, and Billing.

3

Appointments Section

Here you can typically view upcoming and past appointments, and in many cases schedule new ones or request changes to existing bookings.

4

Messages Section

This area allows you to send and receive secure messages with your care team — similar to email but within the secure portal environment.

5

Health & Records Section

This section typically contains visit summaries, test results, health history, and other records that your provider has made available to you.

📋 Features

Common MyChart Features

MyChart offers a range of features that help patients manage their health-related tasks online. The availability of specific features depends on what your healthcare organization has enabled.

📅

Appointment Scheduling

Many organizations allow patients to request or schedule appointments directly through MyChart without calling the office.

📨

Secure Messaging

Patients can send non-urgent questions to their care team and receive responses within the secure portal environment.

🧪

Test Results

Lab and test results are often made available in MyChart for patients to view, sometimes with explanatory notes from the provider.

📄

Visit Summaries

After-visit summaries, care instructions, and documentation from past appointments can typically be viewed and downloaded.

💊

Medication List

MyChart typically displays a list of current and past medications as recorded by your healthcare provider in their system.

💳

Billing & Payments

Many MyChart portals allow patients to view statements and make payments for healthcare services online.

📹

Video Visits

Some organizations use MyChart to host virtual appointments, allowing patients to meet with their provider via video call.

👨‍👩‍👧

Proxy Access

MyChart may allow a parent, caregiver, or trusted person to access and manage a portal on behalf of another individual.

📱 Mobile

Accessing MyChart on Mobile

MyChart can be accessed from smartphones and tablets in two ways — through a mobile web browser or through the official MyChart app.

1

Via Mobile Browser

You can open any mobile browser on your phone or tablet and navigate to your provider's MyChart address. The site is generally mobile-responsive and works without an app.

2

Via the MyChart App

Epic Systems provides an official MyChart app available on both iOS (Apple App Store) and Android (Google Play Store). You can download it and then select your healthcare organization within the app.

3

Select Your Organization

Inside the app, you will be asked to search for and select your specific healthcare organization before signing in with your MyChart credentials.

4

Enable Notifications (Optional)

The app can be configured to send push notifications for new messages, upcoming appointments, or test results becoming available.

ℹ️

PortalPath has no connection to the MyChart app. For app-specific issues, contact your healthcare organization's support team or visit the official app store listing for help.

📞 Support

Getting Real Help With Your MyChart Account

PortalPath is an informational resource only. If you need direct technical support, account access help, or have questions about your specific portal, the right place to go is your healthcare organization directly.

1

Contact Your Healthcare Provider

Your doctor's office, clinic, or hospital manages your MyChart account. Their support team is the correct first point of contact for any account issues.

2

Use the "Help" or "Support" Link on Your Portal

Most MyChart portals have a Help or Contact Us link at the bottom of the page that connects you to the right support channel for your organization.

3

Visit Your Organization's Official Website

Your healthcare provider's website will typically list a patient services phone number or an online support request form for portal-related issues.
